# COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

## Direct Competitors

### Otta (now Welcome to the Jungle)
- **What they do:** Curated job matching for tech/startup roles. Two-way matching.
- **Strengths:** Beautiful UX, transparent salaries, strong brand with younger tech workers.
- **Weaknesses:** Limited to tech/startup. Preference-based, NOT skill-based. Post-acquisition product diluted.
- **Pricing:** Free for seekers. Employers £300-500/role.
- **Gap:** Does not discover roles outside stated preferences. If you don't know a title, Otta won't surface it.

### Eightfold AI
- **What they do:** Enterprise talent intelligence. Deep learning maps skills across entire talent pools.
- **Strengths:** Massive skills taxonomy (1M+ skills), internal mobility, diversity analytics.
- **Weaknesses:** Exclusively B2B enterprise. £100k-500k+/year. Candidates never interact with it.
- **Gap:** All that intelligence locked inside enterprise walls. Individuals can't access it.

### Pymetrics (acquired by Harver)
- **What they do:** Neuroscience games that assess cognitive/emotional traits, match to roles.
- **Strengths:** Bias reduction, academic backing. Used by Unilever, Accenture.
- **Weaknesses:** 25-min game battery. Only works when employer profiles roles. No open marketplace.
- **Gap:** Trait-to-role matching insight never given back to candidate.

### Jobscan / SkillSyncer
- **What they do:** CV keyword optimisation. Match score against a job description.
- **Strengths:** Simple, focused, cheap.
- **Weaknesses:** Requires you to already have a target job. No discovery. Basic keyword matching.
- **Gap:** Optimises applications but never questions whether you're applying to the right roles.

## Adjacent Competitors

### LinkedIn
- **Strengths:** 900M+ members. Rich career trajectory data. AI job summaries.
- **Weaknesses:** Recommendations biased toward current title/industry. Terrible at non-obvious transitions. Endorsements meaningless. Premium mostly about InMails, not intelligence.
- **Gap:** Has the data to do skills-based matching but doesn't. Reinforces existing path.

### Indeed / Reed / Totaljobs
- **Strengths:** Massive job inventory. Strong UK presence.
- **Weaknesses:** Keyword-only matching. No skills intelligence. Flooded with ghost jobs.
- **Gap:** Brute-force keyword matching, no understanding of transferable skills.

### Hired / Hackajob
- **Strengths:** Reverse hiring (companies apply to candidates). Salary transparency.
- **Weaknesses:** Tech-only. US-centric. Senior candidates only.
- **Gap:** Only works if you're already a desirable tech candidate.

### Flexa Careers
- **Strengths:** UK-based flexibility/culture matching. Verified "FlexScore."
- **Weaknesses:** Not skills-based. Small inventory.
- **Gap:** Culture matching is valuable but orthogonal to skills matching.

## What Does NOT Exist Yet

1. **"Skills-First Career Discovery" for individuals** - Nobody offers upload CV > discover unknown roles based on transferable skills
2. **"Career Translation" across industries** - No tool maps skills from one industry to equivalent roles in another for individuals
3. **Salary-transparent, skills-based matching for UK** - Otta was closest, got acquired
4. **"What am I worth?" based on skills, not title** - Current salary tools index on title + location only
5. **Career transition pathways with training gap analysis** - Nobody connects skills gap to specific, costed training paths
6. **Passive career monitoring** - No "set and forget" tool for your skill set
7. **Non-tech, non-graduate matching** - Almost every AI tool built for tech workers only
8. **Informal skills recognition** - No platform recognises YouTube learning, side projects, caring skills

1. Executive Summary

MatchMySkillset is a UK-based career intelligence platform that uses AI to match people to jobs they never knew existed, based on their actual skills rather than their job title history. On the employer side, it surfaces hidden talent that traditional recruitment misses.

The UK recruitment market is worth £141 billion, yet the system is fundamentally broken. Job seekers apply to roles by title, missing thousands of positions their skills perfectly suit. Employers receive hundreds of irrelevant applications while qualified candidates are filtered out by keyword-matching ATS systems. The result: 242 applicants per opening, a 0.4% callback rate, and a £39 billion annual skills gap.

MatchMySkillset solves this from both sides. For job seekers, our AI analyses their skills (from CVs, plain-English descriptions, or even life experience) and maps them to career paths they would never have searched for. For employers, it surfaces pre-qualified candidates whose transferable skills match the role, not just the keywords. For recruitment agents, it provides a tool that makes them dramatically more effective at matching.

Our founding team combines AI technology (Dave) with deep recruitment industry expertise and contacts (Fred). This is not just another tech platform hoping recruiters will show up. Fred's network of employers and candidates provides immediate supply and demand from day one, while the AI layer scales what no human recruiter can do alone.

Our thesis: The most valuable job match is one neither the candidate nor the employer would have found on their own. That is what we build.

2. The Problem

For Job Seekers: A Broken Discovery Engine

Every major job board (LinkedIn, Indeed, Reed, Totaljobs) works the same way: search by job title. This means people only discover roles they already know about. A product manager searches for "product manager." A teacher searches for "teacher." Nobody searches for "learning experience designer" or "customer success manager" because they have never heard of those roles, despite having every skill required.

"This job market is terrifying. It's a black hole that makes you question everything."
Reddit r/ukjobs, 2025

The numbers tell the story:

  • 242 applicants per opening (3x the 2017 rate), with a 0.4% callback rate
  • 75% of applicants receive zero response to their applications
  • 73% of applicants lack required qualifications because they are applying to the wrong roles
  • 66% of ATS systems cannot understand synonyms ("Project Management" does not match "Program Management")
  • 20-30% of all job listings are ghost jobs with no intention to hire
  • 97.8% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS that rejects 75% of CVs before a human sees them
"Almost nobody wants to train or bring anyone on anymore. If you don't have the exact job title, you're invisible."
Mumsnet, Careers forum, 2025

For Employers: Drowning in Noise, Starving for Talent

Employers face the mirror image of this problem. AI application tools have made spray-and-pray applications trivially easy, flooding inboxes with irrelevant CVs while genuinely qualified candidates (especially career changers with transferable skills) are invisible.

  • 1,200-2,000 applications per role, with over 90% completely unqualified
  • £6,125 average cost per hire, rising to £19,000 for management roles
  • 95% of UK businesses admit to making at least one bad hire per year
  • £132,000 average cost of a bad hire at mid-manager level
  • 42+ days average time to fill a vacancy
  • 75% of UK firms struggle to find staff with the right skills
  • 62% of SMEs cannot operate at full capacity due to hiring difficulties
  • £39 billion annual UK cost of the skills mismatch
"We get 1,500 applications for every role. Maybe 50 are relevant. Our ATS can't tell the difference, and my team doesn't have time to read them all."
UK HR Director, Reddit r/humanresources

For Recruitment Agents: Squeezed from All Sides

  • 181 UK recruitment agencies entered liquidation in H1 2025 (18% increase YoY)
  • Employer NICs rising to 15%, squeezing margins
  • Clients pushing back on 15-20% placement fees
  • LinkedIn Recruiter costs £9,000/seat/year with only 4-25% InMail response rates
  • In-house teams poaching agency staff, then only sending agencies the hardest-to-fill roles
  • AI tools threatening to disintermediate the entire agency model
The core insight: The recruitment industry optimises for job titles and keywords. The real world runs on skills. This mismatch costs the UK economy £39 billion per year. MatchMySkillset bridges that gap.

4. The Solution

MatchMySkillset is built on three pillars:

Pillar 1: AI-Powered Skills Intelligence

Our AI engine does what no job board offers: it understands skills, not keywords. It extracts skills from CVs, plain-English descriptions, and even life experience (parenting, volunteering, hobbies). It then maps those skills against thousands of job roles using O*NET, ESCO, and our proprietary adjacency model built from real career transition data.

Pillar 2: Human-Led Guidance

AI discovers the possibilities. A human recruiter validates them. Fred and our team of specialist career transition recruiters intervene at three critical points: career direction validation, application review, and offer negotiation. This is not a chatbot pretending to be a career advisor. This is real expertise layered on top of AI intelligence.

Pillar 3: Two-Sided Marketplace

Job seekers find roles they never knew existed. Employers find candidates they never thought to look for. The platform creates matches that neither side would have discovered alone, then Fred's team ensures those matches convert into hires.

The tagline: AI-Assisted. Human-Led. Your career, discovered.

5. Bootstrap Strategy: How We Start

Every marketplace faces the chicken-and-egg problem: candidates will not come without jobs, and employers will not come without candidates. We solve this by not requiring employers to sign up at all in the early stages. Instead, we use free job APIs for immediate job inventory, a viral free tool to attract CVs, and Fred's recruitment expertise to take those CVs directly to employers.

The key insight: We do not need employers on the platform. We need CVs. Fred IS the employer side. He takes the candidates to employers. The platform is his superpower.

Step 1: The Hook (No Jobs Needed)

The viral entry point is NOT "find a job." It is "Discover careers you never knew existed."

A free tool, no sign-up required. Paste your CV or describe your experience. AI extracts your skills and shows you 5-10 career paths you have never heard of, with salary ranges, transition difficulty, and skills gaps. This is shareable, interesting, and useful even without a single job listing. People will do it out of curiosity. "I had no idea I was a 92% match for UX Researcher" is the kind of thing people screenshot and share on LinkedIn, Reddit, and WhatsApp.

That is how we get CVs. People come for the free insight. We capture their skill profile when they create an account to save their results.

Step 2: Jobs from APIs (Immediate Inventory)

From day one, the platform has thousands of real UK jobs by aggregating free and revenue-share job APIs. Users get genuine value: skill-matched job recommendations from real listings, ranked by how well their transferable skills match each role.

Step 3: Fred's Direct Employer Outreach + Reverse Recruitment

Fred works both sides from day one:

A) Featured Jobs: Fred Brings Employers to the Platform

Fred approaches employers in his network directly and offers them something no job board does: "Post your role with us, and instead of 1,500 random applications, you will receive a curated shortlist of 10-15 candidates whose skills genuinely match, verified by AI and reviewed by me personally."

These become Featured Jobs on the platform, displayed prominently above API-sourced listings. Employers get a premium, curated experience. Job seekers see real, exclusive roles alongside the API jobs. Fred charges employers either a listing fee (£199-499) or works on a placement basis (15-20% of salary). This gives the platform exclusive job content from day one that no other aggregator has.

B) Reverse Recruitment: Fred Takes Candidates to Employers

Simultaneously, Fred has a growing pool of skill-profiled candidates (far richer than raw CVs) that the AI has matched against real job listings. Every week, Fred:

  1. Reviews the platform's strongest new candidates (AI flags the top 10 with best transferable skills)
  2. Picks 5 to work with personally
  3. Matches them against live roles (API data + Featured Jobs + his own network)
  4. For Featured Jobs: sends the employer a curated shortlist with skills-match breakdowns
  5. For external roles: approaches the employer directly with pre-screened candidates
  6. Charges placement fees (15-20% of salary) on successful hires

This two-pronged approach means Fred is both bringing jobs to the candidates (Featured Jobs) and bringing candidates to the jobs (reverse recruitment). The platform has exclusive content, employers get a premium service, and candidates see curated opportunities alongside the API aggregated listings.

Step 4: The Flywheel Kicks In

Once Fred places a few people, everything accelerates:

  • Case studies: "Sarah was a teacher. We matched her to UX Research. She was hired in 8 weeks." This is the content that drives more CVs.
  • Employer relationships: Companies Fred placed with now trust the platform and sign up directly (becoming subscription revenue).
  • Word of mouth: Placed candidates tell friends. Success stories spread on social media.
  • Data: Every placement validates the matching algorithm, making it more accurate.
  • Transition Twins: Successfully placed candidates become mentors for the next wave.

6. How It Works: Job Seekers

The User Journey

Step 1: Tell Us Who You Are

Upload your CV, paste your LinkedIn profile, or simply describe what you have done in plain English. Our AI extracts every skill, including ones you did not know you had. "I organised a charity fundraiser for 200 people" becomes: Event Management, Budget Management, Team Leadership, Stakeholder Communication.

Step 2: Discover Careers You Never Knew Existed

The AI maps your skills against thousands of roles and surfaces 5-10 career paths ranked by match strength, salary potential, and transition difficulty. A teacher discovers they are a 92% match for UX Researcher. A project manager in construction learns they suit Product Manager in tech. A retail manager finds they match Operations Analyst.

Step 3: See Your Career GPS

For each suggested path, see three routes:

  • Express Route: Fastest path, may require intensive upskilling (timeline, cost, courses)
  • Scenic Route: Stepping-stone roles that build experience gradually
  • Wildcard Route: Unexpected paths that others with your profile took successfully

Step 4: Close the Gap

For your chosen path, see exactly what skills you need to develop, with specific courses (free and paid options), estimated time commitment, and how each one improves your match score in real time.

Step 5: Get Matched and Supported

When you are ready, get matched with real job openings. A human recruiter reviews your application strategy. You join a cohort of others making similar transitions. You connect with a "Transition Twin" mentor who has already made your exact career change.

7. How It Works: Employers

The Employer Pain We Solve

Employers waste thousands of pounds and weeks of time sifting through irrelevant applications, while the perfect candidate, someone from a different industry with exactly the right transferable skills, never applies because they did not know the role existed.

The Employer Journey

Step 1: Post Your Role (or Describe It)

Upload a job description or describe the role in plain language. Our AI extracts the underlying skills required, not just the keywords you happened to use. It also flags where your job description might be inadvertently excluding great candidates (jargon, unnecessary requirements, biased language).

Step 2: See Pre-Qualified Matches

Instead of receiving 1,500 unfiltered applications, get a shortlist of 10-20 candidates whose actual skills match your actual needs. Each candidate shows: match score, transferable skills breakdown, skills gaps (with how they are closing them), and a "Verified Potential" profile with skill demonstrations.

Step 3: Blind Matching (Optional)

Review candidate skill profiles without seeing names, photos, previous job titles, or educational background. Reduce bias. Discover talent you would have unconsciously dismissed. Only see full profiles after expressing interest.

Step 4: Human Validation

Fred's team validates the shortlist, provides context on each candidate's transition readiness, and manages the introduction. This is not a job board where you post and pray. This is curated matching with a human guarantee.

10. Unique Differentiators

1. Skills-First Discovery (not title-first search)

Every competitor starts with "what job do you want?" We start with "what can you do?" This is a fundamentally different approach that unlocks career paths invisible to traditional search.

2. AI-Assisted, Human-Led

AI handles the 90% that scales (skills extraction, matching, gap analysis). A human recruiter handles the 10% that matters most (career direction validation, application strategy, offer negotiation). No other platform combines both.

3. Fred's Network (the unfair advantage)

Our co-founder is an active recruitment agent with a deep network of employers and candidates. This solves the chicken-and-egg problem from day one. The "Hidden Jobs" feature (unadvertised roles from Fred's network) is something no tech-only platform can replicate.

4. Two-Sided Value

Most platforms serve candidates OR employers. We create value for both simultaneously. Every candidate we match is one fewer irrelevant application an employer has to process. Every employer listing is one more opportunity for a career changer.

5. Life Skills Recognition

Our AI recognises skills from parenting, volunteering, caring, and side projects. A stay-at-home parent returning to work is not "unemployed for 5 years." They have project management, crisis resolution, budget management, and stakeholder negotiation skills. We see that. No ATS does.

6. Transition Twins (mentor matching)

We match career changers with people who have already made the exact same transition. A teacher who became a UX designer mentors a teacher considering UX. This peer network is a moat that compounds over time.

  • Backend: Supabase (auth, database, real-time), Vercel (hosting, edge functions)
  • AI Engine: Claude API for skills extraction, career matching, and natural language processing
  • Skills Ontology: O*NET + ESCO as base, augmented with proprietary adjacency model
  • Job Data: Adzuna API, Reed API, Indeed Publisher, Jooble (free UK job feeds providing instant inventory)
  • Labour Market Data: UK ONS, ASHE salary statistics, Lightcast job posting data
  • Payments: Stripe (subscriptions, one-off purchases, marketplace payouts)

AI Architecture

  1. Skills Extraction Engine: Parses CVs, LinkedIn profiles, and plain-text descriptions. Extracts skills, assigns confidence scores, maps to standardised taxonomy.
  2. Career Matching Algorithm: Maps extracted skills against role requirements. Uses adjacency scoring (how close is skill A to required skill B?) rather than keyword matching.
  3. Gap Analysis Engine: Identifies missing skills per target role. Recommends specific courses with time/cost estimates. Updates match scores in real time as users complete learning.
  4. Transition Probability Model: Built from anonymised career transition data. Shows which career moves succeed, how long they take, and what differentiates successful transitions.

Data Moat

Every user interaction improves the matching algorithm. Every successful placement validates the model. Over time, we build the UK's most comprehensive dataset on career transitions, skills adjacency, and what actually predicts successful hires. This data moat compounds and becomes increasingly difficult to replicate.

18. Risks & Mitigations

RiskImpactMitigation
Chicken-and-egg: need candidates to attract employers and vice versa High Fred's network provides immediate supply on both sides. Start with niche focus (3 transition types) rather than trying to be everything.
LinkedIn builds similar features High LinkedIn has had this data for years and hasn't built it. Their incentive is engagement, not career transitions (which reduce engagement). Our human-led approach and niche focus are defensible.
AI matching accuracy is poor initially Medium Fred's human validation catches AI errors. Start with constrained niches where training data is richer. Feedback loops improve the model rapidly.
Candidates won't pay Medium LinkedIn Premium growing 50% proves willingness. Multiple free-to-paid conversion points. Employer revenue doesn't depend on candidates paying.
Regulatory change (AI hiring legislation) Medium Human-in-the-loop model is specifically what regulators recommend. We are positioned on the right side of any AI hiring regulation.
Fred leaves / key person risk High Document processes. Build employer relationships at company level, not personal level. Hire additional recruiters early.
Data quality / matching errors damage trust High Human review of all matches in early stages. Quality guarantee: bad match = money back. Transparent matching logic.

AI-Assisted. Human-Led. The future of recruitment is not about replacing humans with algorithms. It is about giving humans the intelligence to make better decisions. Fred brings the expertise. The AI brings the scale. Together, we build something neither could achieve alone.
